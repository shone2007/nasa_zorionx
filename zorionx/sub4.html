NASA Space Radiation Laboratory (NSRL)

The NASA Space Radiation Laboratory (NSRL), situated at Brookhaven National Laboratory (BNL) in Upton, New York, serves as the premier facility for simulating the deep-space radiation environment. It is a vital resource for mitigating the major health risks associated with long-duration human space missions beyond low-Earth orbit, including the Artemis program to the Moon and missions to Mars.

It is the only facility in the United States capable of delivering the diverse ion beams necessary to accurately simulate the complex mix of cosmic rays and solar particles encountered outside Earth's protective magnetosphere.

The primary mission of the NSRL is to provide beams of high-energy charged particles to characterize the biological and physical risks to astronauts. This requires simulating two major components of space radiation:

  • Galactic Cosmic Rays (GCR): High-energy protons and heavy ions that originate from outside the solar system
  • Solar Particle Events (SPE): Large bursts of protons and heavier ions ejected from the Sun

Research Capabilities:

  • Biological Studies: Analyzing the impact of radiation on living systems, including DNA, cells, and organs
  • Countermeasure Testing: Evaluating new radiation shielding materials for spacecraft and habitats
  • Pharmaceutical Research: Testing radioprotectants that might help astronauts recover from radiation effects

The work at NSRL directly supports NASA's Human Research Program by addressing critical risks:

  • Radiation Exposure: Long-term issues like cancer and cardiovascular disease
  • Central Nervous System Impairment: Impacts on cognition, motor control, and behavior
  • Altered Gravity and Hostile Environments: Overall risk profile for crew performance
  • Medical and Behavioral Health: Effective countermeasures for physical and psychological effects
